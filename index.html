Deepak Raina


I am an Assitant Professor at the Center for Artifical Intelligence and Robotics (CAIR) at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Mandi, which is located in scenic mountains of Himachal Pradesh, India. My research is centered around Robotics, Computer Vision, and Deep Learning, with a focus on their applications in healthcare.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Johns Hopkins Malone Center for Engineering in Healthcare with Prof. Axel Krieger and Prof. Muyinatu Bell. In my postdoctoral, I developed advanced robotic manipulation and imaging guidance methods for ultrasound-guided surgical interventions. I obtained Ph.D. in 2024 from IIT Delhi advised by Prof. Subir K. Saha and Prof. Richard Voyles, with a significant portion of my research conducted at Purdue University as a visiting student. My doctoral research focused on autonomous robotic ultrasound system for diagnostic imaging, specifically focusing on imitation learning for robot manipulation and deep learning for medical imaging analysis. Notably, the telerobotic ultrasound system was developed, with which 21 human trials were conducted at AIIMS, Delhi. Prior to joining Ph.D., I was a researcher at TCS Robotics Research and Innovation Lab (Delhi), devloping robotic solutions for warehouse tasks (palletizing, truck loading, etc.), one of which was patented and considered for commercial launch. I obtained my M.Tech at IIT Jodhpur in 2017, advised by Prof. Suril V. Shah, where my research focused on space robotics for satellete servicing and capturing debris.

I was awarded three prestigious fellowships: the Prime Minister's Research Fellowship (PMRF) at IIT Delhi, SERB-Overseas Visiting Doctoral Fellowship (OVDF) at Purdue University, and Malone Postdoctoral Fellowship (MPDF) at Johns Hopkins. My work has been published in top-tier conferences such as ICRA, CASE, ISMR, and ROMAN, as well as renowned IEEE Transactions, including TMRB and TUFFC.

Beyond research, I enjoy staying active through badminton, strength training, yoga, hiking and running. Staying active and connected with nature keeps me energized and ready for research challenges.

Research

To unlock the full potential of robotics in complex medical procedures, my research focuses on advancing methods in medical imaging guidance and robotic manipulation that will allow robots to learn by observing clincal experts. It simplifies the learning process and eventually lets these robots perceive and operate like clincal experts in diagnostics and surgical interventions.

My ongoing research is centered around the following themes:

  1. Visuomotor skill learning through observation: Clincal experts synthesize feedback from the medical image and combine it with their knowledge of the anatomical structure(s) to execute these continuous corrections to the surgical tool. Inspired by this process, I aim to develop an end-to-end model that directly maps these tooling motions to the imaging perception space., eliminating the need for analytical engineereed procedure that often vary across patients.

  2. Coaching through multi-modal feedback: Expert instruction often includes multi-modal cues such as visual, kinesthetic, and verbal. My research aims to build non-linear multi-modal mapping that can interpret and translate such cues into actionable guidance for robots, enabling them to learn from expert feedback in real time (i.e. coaching).

  3. Imaging guidance for medical robots: I am developing deep learning models capable of holistic interpretation of medical imaging to extract low-dimensional, meaningful feedback. This feedback is designed to integrate seamlessly with robot learning frameworks, enhancing their ability to make informed decisions during procedures.

  4. High-fidelity data collection: A foundational element of my research is collecting high-quality data from clinical experts. I am improving data collection interfaces using advanced teleoperation architectures, including augmented/virtual reality, haptic feedback, and advanced control systems, to capture expert performance with high fidelity.

I am a graduate (Ph.D.) candidate under a prestigious Prime Minister's Research Fellow (PMRF) scheme at IIT Delhi advised by Prof. Subir Kumar Saha. Currently, I am a visiting Ph.D. student at Collaborative Robotics Lab, Purdue University, USA, where I am advised by Prof. Richard Voyles. My research interests lie at the intersection of medical robotics, sensing and imaging, with a particular focus on developing autonomous systems for precise and safe patient diagnosis. My current research projects include designing a robotic-assisted ultrasound imaging system, developing ultrasound imaging analysis techniques, and building precise tele-robotic systems for diagnostic healthcare. I am deeply passionate about developing innovative technologies that can significantly improve medical care and treatment outcomes.

Prior to joining Ph.D., I was a researcher at TCS Robotics Research and Innovation Lab (Delhi), contributing towards the automation of warehouse industry under the supervision of Dr. Swagat Kumar and Mr. Rajesh Sinha. I obtained M.Tech at IIT Jodhpur in 2017 advised by Prof. Suril V. Shah. My master's research focused on space robotics, addressing autonomous on-orbit servicing (OOS) operations such as capture of debris and servicing of satellite using a multi-arm robot.
